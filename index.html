The Challenge

Sonset Solutions has developed a portable, solar-powered radio for use by the 1.2 billion people without access to electricity. The radio is tuned to the frequency of SonSet’s local ministry partners, with the goal of bringing Christian radio to people who aren’t easily reachable otherwise. SonSet now wishes to augment their radio with offline audio Bibles in different languages. Our task is to write human-facing software that SonSet’s partners can use to easily load content onto SD cards, which will then be inserted into the radios.

SonSet’s partners operate in a number of different countries and use a variety of languages. Our software needs to run on all the popular operating systems (Windows, macOS, Linux) and support English, French, and Spanish.

Project Schedule

Phase 1

Research

After viewing our given instructions for the project, our team’s first approach was research. We picked research topics based on questions that arose from our first client meeting. We primarily focused on localization, cross-platform solutions, a drag & drop file system, audio transformation, database management, and risk analysis information. The research provided a lot of clarity for our team on how to begin developing our requirements for the project.

Phase 2

Requirements

We began with the requirements that were given to us by SonSet Solutions. These included the following application capabilities:

  • Cross-platform functionality
  • Able to download content from SonSet’s servers
  • Able to write data to SD card
  • Accounting information tracking
  • Multi-language interface
  • Audio format conversion

Our team broke these down further into low-level required and desired requirements. Use cases and mockups were created to explore how these requirements would be implemented in our system.

Phase 3

Design

After fleshing out our requirements, we then began exploring how to implement them in our application design. Our app will be built using NW.js, a framework for creating native apps. Due to the modular nature of our system features, we decided implement our solution using a functional approach rather than an object-oriented one. This paradigm also allows us to take advantage of existing JavaScript capabilities.
